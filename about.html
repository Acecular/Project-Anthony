    Hi! Thank you for visiting and welcome to my website! I am currently a second year undergraduate attending UC Berkeley majoring in Molecular Biology while also pursuing a Computer Science minor. My goal is to one day study and practice medicine while also incorporating technology into helping make the field of medicine more streamline and efficient.

    As a student of UC Berkeley, I am involved in a variety of extracurricular activities that I am passionate about.

    An organization I am apart of is the Associated Students of the University of California (ASUC) which is the largest and most autonomous student association in the country. Starting in August 2016, I have served as the Director of Accountability under Chief Accountability Officer Kenny Chen. Our office strives to hold all elected and appointed officials accountable for their actions, platforms, and duties to the student body. We work closely with the Chief Legal Officer/Attorney General and the Judicial Council to maintain and enforce internal accountability within the ASUC and the student body. Every week, I also verify that all executive officers, appointed officials, senators, and commission chairs keep up with their attendance at committee and Senate meetings as well as outreach requirements. Weekly reports and mid-year reports are also due to our office where we review the elected officials' activities and accomplishments during their terms to ensure that they have been continually productive. Through these reports, our office also spearheads the ASUC Transparency Initiative Project by providing public access to ASUC official documents which include financial records, performance reviews, ASUC archives, and weekly reports.

    One of the greatest joys of being a part of the Berkeley community is to give back to the community around us. At Cal, I am a part of the Oakland Asian Student Education Services (OASES) where I spend three hours a week tutoring and mentoring a class of underprivileged students at Lincoln Elementary School as a volunteer. I help them with homework, learning, and goal-setting. I review concepts which may have given them trouble in school and help them to learn the material instead of giving them the answers. In Fall 2016, I mentored a group of fifth graders. It is one of my greatest joys to see these young children grow from the first day I mentored them to now. I know that they will one day grow up to be successful and giving people. I also regularly participate in the Berkeley Project which is a large scale community service throughout the city of Berkeley.

    When I am back home in Fremont, California, I enjoy doing a variety of activities that keep me going and active. I regularly volunteer at Washington Hospital as a Surgery Liaison where I keep concerned friends and family of surgery patients updated on the progress of the procedure. I serve as an intermediary role between the nurses/doctors and friends/family in the surgery waiting room. Occasionally, I am also back at ClubSport Fremont where I serve as a lifeguard and as a coach for the Flying Fish Swim Team.
