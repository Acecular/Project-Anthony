    "The Associated Students of the University of California (ASUC) is the officially recognized student association at the University of California, Berkeley. Known as one of the largest and most autonomous student organizations in the nation, the ASUC is an independent 501(c)3 nonprofit organization separate from University governance. In addition to controlling funding for student clubs and organizations, providing resources and student programming, overseeing commercial activities and student services including the Cal Student Store and Lower Sproul Plaza in partnership with the ASUC Student Union, the ASUC advocates for students on a University, local, state, and national level and represents the student body on administrative campus committees."

    Spring 2017

    I continued this semester as the Director of Accountability in the Office of Accountability. We began this semester with a launching of our new website at accountability.asuc.org . It was developed by our Director of Operations and maintains an efficient and organized location for all of the Association officials' reports. Previously, there was a problem accessing archived reports but I believe that with this new system that this problem can be solved. Officials could submit reports through this system and a punctuality chart is also shown regarding the timeliness of each officials' report. This proved more effective than the Google Forms we used last semester and provided easier public access. With elections season, the Accountability Office played a role in maintaining a just and fair election. Bylaws were enforced by our office in preserving the integrity of the ASUC elections. Students were encouraged to report any violations to our office and we also played an active role in closely monitoring campaigns. Measures were taken and enforced by the Accountability Office, the Legal Office, and the Elections Council with all decisions handled by the Judicial Council of the ASUC.

    Fall 2016

    This semester I had the privilege of being in the Office of Accountability as the Director of Accountability. My goals were to primarily enforce attendance at committee and Senate meetings. I made sure weekly and bi-weekly reports were submitted for executive officials, appointed officials, and senators that accurately reflected what our officials were doing for the student body/campus. These reports were then compiled into a centralized location and made public as part of the ASUC Transparency Initiative Projecy. Along with the Chief Accountability Officer, I took part in meetings with officials regarding performance and progress on the platforms they ran their elections on. I also sat in on Governance and Internal Affairs and Senate Meetings to discuss Senate Resolutions, interviewed potential nominees for Judicial Council and ensured that elected officials were held accountable for what they promised to the students. At the end of the semester, I helped gather and arrange the stipends for the 40+ association officials based on their performance throughout the semester. The mid-year report was also due to our office at the end of the semester.
